# Randomware demo script (final video: 1:55)

The submitted video was assembled from a single silent owner screen capture plus two short browser-recorded inserts, with narration synthesized by macOS text-to-speech (voice: Daniel). The build-wait section is presented as a labeled time-lapse (the on-screen ELAPSED counter races) rather than being hidden — real builds take minutes and the video says so.

**0:00–0:31 — framing and spin (normal speed).** "Randomware is a slot machine for software. A human owned the product direction; GPT-5.6 Sol designed the safety contract, and GPT-5.6 Luna implemented it in one Codex goal session, under a one-hundred-dollar credit budget. I press Spin: two bounded public APIs collide — a card deck, and dog photos. The model gets a collision, not a template."

**0:31–0:41 — build wait (8× time-lapse).** "The model invents a concept and composes a complete single-file app. Real builds take minutes — this one even needed its one honest repair."

**0:41–0:53 — publication (2×).** "The specimen is validated — byte range, markers, literal broker calls — then published with its own record page."

**0:53–1:41 — live result (normal speed).** "This is Pawns and Paws: a drawn card sets the movement rule for a newly summoned dog pawn. Every draw calls the server-side broker for real data — a fresh card, a fresh dog — and the sandboxed app can reach nothing else. Source, requests, and dataflow stay inspectable on every specimen."

**1:41–1:48 — showcase and second result.** "The public showcase needs zero setup — and spinning again yields a different collision every time."

**1:48–1:55 — close on an honest failure.** "Failure is a specimen too: an honest death certificate. Real APIs go in; random apps come out."
